# Healthcare Staffing Demand Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
Analyzed influenza-related mortality and population data to estimate healthcare staffing demand across U.S. states. The goal was to identify high-risk populations, anticipate peak demand periods, and support proactive workforce planning for the influenza season.

## 🎯 Business Objectives
- Analyze staffing demand trends over time  
- Identify peak demand periods and potential understaffing risks  
- Support data-driven workforce planning and scheduling decisions  
- Prioritize resource allocation based on vulnerable populations  

## 🗂️ Data Sources
- CDC Influenza Mortality Data (2009–2017)  
  - Includes death counts by geography and age group  
- U.S. Census Bureau Data (2009–2017)  
  - Population demographics by state, age, and gender  

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Excel (data analysis and reporting)  
- Tableau (data visualization and dashboards)  
- Descriptive statistics and trend analysis  

## 🔍 Analytical Approach
- Cleaned and validated multi-source datasets  
- Analyzed mortality trends across time and geography  
- Segmented populations by age to identify vulnerable groups  
- Compared population distribution with mortality rates  
- Built visualizations to highlight demand patterns  

## 📊 Key Insights
- Influenza-related mortality is significantly higher among elderly populations (65+), indicating increased staffing demand for high-risk groups  
- Demand shows clear seasonal patterns, with peak periods requiring increased staffing coverage to maintain service levels  
- Geographic variation in mortality and population distribution highlights the need for region-specific staffing strategies  
- Data-driven staffing models can reduce understaffing risk and improve resource allocation during peak flu season

## 💡 Recommendations
- Prioritize staffing in states with high concentrations of vulnerable populations  
- Allocate additional resources during peak influenza months (October–February)  
- Expand risk criteria to include all individuals aged 65+  
- Develop training programs focused on elderly patient care  
- Integrate additional data sources (hospital admissions, ER visits) to improve forecasting accuracy  
- Implement ongoing monitoring to adjust staffing levels in real time  

## 🚀 Next Steps
- Develop predictive models to forecast staffing demand  
- Incorporate more recent data for improved accuracy  
- Enhance segmentation using additional demographic and health indicators
